Case Study — 02

Goblin

Building a scalable design system from scratch to align three product squads and transform how a fast-growing SaaS team ships UI.

Role Product Designer
Timeline 8 Weeks
Platform Web · SaaS
Year 2024
When three squads are building independently, inconsistency isn't a style problem — it's a product problem. Goblin needed a single source of truth to ship with speed and confidence.
The Challenge

Three squads, three
different products.

Goblin had scaled from a single product team to three independent squads in under a year. Without a shared system, each squad had built its own components — leading to over 200 divergent UI elements, visual inconsistencies visible to users, and a design-to-dev handoff process that averaged 3 days per feature.

The Problem

Designers were spending 40% of their time rebuilding components that already existed elsewhere. Engineers were making style decisions they shouldn't have had to make. The result was a fragmented product experience that eroded user trust.

Design Goals

  • Establish one shared Figma component library
  • Reduce handoff time from 3 days to same-day
  • Create a token-based system extensible by engineering
  • Document usage patterns, not just visuals
Audit & Discovery

Finding what
already existed.

Before designing anything new, I conducted a full UI audit across all three product surfaces — cataloguing every button, form field, modal, and card variant in use. The audit surfaced 47 distinct button styles and 12 competing type scales. The problem was bigger than expected.

I ran collaborative workshops with design and engineering leads from each squad to understand working patterns, pain points, and what "done" looked like. This shaped the system's contribution model as much as its visual language.

Audit Scope

  • 3 product surfaces reviewed end-to-end
  • 200+ existing components catalogued
  • Workshops with 9 cross-functional stakeholders
  • Benchmark review of 5 mature design systems

Key Findings

  • No shared colour or spacing tokens existed
  • Components were built for context, not reuse
  • Figma files were used as delivery, not source of truth
  • Engineers wanted live usage examples, not just specs
The Solution

One library.
Every squad.

The Goblin Design System launched with 120 production-ready components organised into five tiers: tokens, primitives, components, patterns, and templates. Every component included usage guidelines, accessibility notes, and do/don't examples.

A contribution model let individual squads propose additions via a lightweight RFC process — keeping the system alive without creating a bottleneck. The magenta brand accent was applied sparingly through a clear hierarchy, giving the product a distinct visual personality without sacrificing readability.

Outcomes

Faster ships,
fewer fires.

Within three months of launch, all three squads had adopted the system. Design-to-dev handoff that once took days was happening the same afternoon.

60% Reduction in design-to-dev handoff time across all squads
90% Component adoption rate across product teams in 3 months
120 Production-ready components shipped at launch
