Mobile Fintech Prototyping
Case Study — 03

Tinubu

Designing a mobile-first personal finance app that makes understanding your money feel simple, human, and trustworthy — not overwhelming.

Role UX Designer
Timeline 10 Weeks
Platform iOS · Android
Year 2023
Money is emotional. People don't just want to see their balance — they want to feel in control of it. Tinubu was designed to create that feeling from the first screen.
The Challenge

Finance apps that
feel like homework.

Most personal finance apps overwhelm users with graphs, categories, and budgeting tools before they've had a chance to understand their own financial picture. The result is a 68% drop-off at onboarding and low long-term retention — a pattern repeated across the category.

The Problem

Early user testing revealed that newcomers to Tinubu felt anxious rather than empowered when they first opened the app. The existing flow asked for too much too soon and gave too little clarity in return. Trust was never established.

Design Goals

  • Increase onboarding completion to above 80%
  • Make total balance the first thing users understand
  • Design for financial anxiety, not financial expertise
  • Keep the core experience to three key screens
Research & Discovery

Money diaries &
real behaviour.

I ran diary studies with 12 participants over two weeks — asking them to note every time they thought about money, checked a balance, or avoided checking one. The data revealed that anxiety, not lack of interest, was the primary barrier to engagement.

Follow-up interviews uncovered a consistent pattern: users wanted a single, trusted number first. Everything else — categories, trends, goals — was secondary context they'd seek out when ready.

Methods Used

  • 2-week diary studies with 12 participants
  • In-depth interviews on financial behaviour
  • Usability testing across 3 prototype rounds
  • Competitive teardown of 6 finance apps

Key Findings

  • Users checked balance daily but avoided detail
  • Green as a colour consistently signalled safety
  • Complex graphs triggered avoidance, not engagement
  • Trust was built through clarity, not features
The Solution

One number.
Total clarity.

Tinubu's redesign centres on a single "Total Balance" view with progressive disclosure — the big number is always visible, and detail is one tap away. Green as the primary brand colour was a deliberate trust signal, rooted directly in research findings.

Transaction history is surfaced chronologically rather than categorised, reflecting how users actually think about their spending. Growth indicators and active user counts give the dashboard a sense of momentum without requiring manual input.

Outcomes

Users who actually
come back.

Post-launch data confirmed that simplicity was the right bet. Onboarding completion climbed significantly, and daily check-ins became a habit — not a chore.

74% Increase in onboarding completion rate post-redesign
4.8 App Store rating from users in the first 60 days
3× Increase in daily check-ins compared to previous version
