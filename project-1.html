Case Study — 01

Bayse UX-Improvement

Redesigning the core experience of Bayse — a Nigerian prediction market app — to make discovering, understanding, and placing trades faster and more intuitive.

Role Product Designer
Timeline 8 Weeks
Platform iOS · Android
Year 2024
Prediction markets are only as powerful as their UX. If users can't find a market, understand the odds, or place a trade with confidence — the product fails, no matter how smart the underlying platform is.
The Challenge

A powerful product
users couldn't navigate.

Bayse is a Nigerian prediction market app that lets users trade on real-world outcomes — from politics and sports to crypto and social media. The platform had strong content and an engaged niche community, but a UX that made discovery difficult and the trade flow confusing for new users.

The Problem

New users arriving on the home screen struggled to understand what Bayse was and how to participate. The navigation categories (Updates, Leaderboard, Prediction Market, How to Deposit) weren't intuitive, and the market feed lacked the context needed to make confident trades.

Design Goals

  • Reduce time from open to first trade placed
  • Make market categories immediately scannable
  • Clarify Yes/No odds display for new users
  • Improve onboarding and first-session retention
Research & Discovery

Understanding the
prediction mindset.

I conducted usability testing sessions with both existing Bayse users and people unfamiliar with prediction markets. The sessions revealed two distinct mental models: experienced users who thought in terms of odds and volume, and newcomers who simply wanted to back their opinion on familiar topics like Nigerian politics and football.

A competitive review of global prediction market apps — including Polymarket and Kalshi — helped identify patterns in market card design, odds presentation, and category navigation that could be adapted for the Bayse audience.

Methods Used

  • Moderated usability testing (8 sessions)
  • First-click testing on navigation patterns
  • Competitive analysis of 5 prediction apps
  • User interviews on trade decision-making

Key Findings

  • Users skipped the category nav — fed by the stream
  • Yes/No pricing wasn't understood by first-timers
  • Social context (trades, volume) built confidence
  • Trending tab was the most valuable entry point
The Solution

Clarity first,
then confidence.

The redesign prioritised the home feed as the primary discovery surface — elevating the Trending tab and adding clearer market cards that showed trade volume, end conditions, and simplified Yes/No pricing. Category icons were redesigned with clearer labels and a horizontal scroll that didn't compete with the feed.

A balance indicator (₦10,990) was made persistent at the top of every screen — a small change that had an outsized impact on how safe and in-control users felt while browsing markets.

Outcomes

More users trading,
sooner.

Post-redesign testing showed significant improvements in first-session engagement and trade conversion — confirming that clarity, not more features, was the lever that mattered.

43% Increase in first-session trade conversion rate
2× Improvement in new user retention after 7 days
↑ 196 Active daily trades tracked post-launch on redesigned feed
