UX Research Product Design Web App
Case Study — 01

Makins

Redesigning a B2B analytics dashboard to help teams monitor performance at a glance — reducing cognitive load and surfacing what actually matters.

Role Lead Product Designer
Timeline 12 Weeks
Platform Web · SaaS
Year 2024
Analytics tools are powerful — but only when users can find the signal in the noise. Makins needed a dashboard that made decisions obvious, not effortful.
The Challenge

A dashboard drowning
in its own data.

Makins had grown rapidly, and its dashboard had grown with it — but without direction. Over 40 metrics were displayed simultaneously with no visual hierarchy, no prioritisation, and no way to personalise the view. Power users knew where to look; everyone else was lost.

The Problem

Users spent an average of 4.2 minutes on the dashboard before taking any action. Session recordings showed erratic scrolling, repeated backtracking, and frequent exports to spreadsheets — a sign the tool wasn't answering their real questions.

Design Goals

  • Reduce time-to-insight to under 2 minutes
  • Surface the three metrics each user cares most about
  • Increase daily active usage by 30%
  • Make onboarding for new users self-serve
Research & Discovery

Listening before
designing anything.

I ran 14 user interviews across three customer segments — founders, marketing managers, and operations leads. Each group had a fundamentally different mental model of what "a good dashboard" meant. Founders wanted high-level health indicators. Ops leads wanted drill-down accuracy. Marketers wanted trend lines.

Card-sorting exercises revealed that users grouped information by decision type rather than metric category — a key insight that restructured the entire information architecture.

Methods Used

  • 14 moderated user interviews
  • Session recording analysis (Hotjar)
  • Card sorting with 20 participants
  • Competitive analysis of 8 dashboards

Key Findings

  • Users ignored 70% of visible metrics daily
  • Top 3 metrics differed per role
  • Charts without context caused confusion
  • Mobile access was more common than expected
The Solution

Three goals.
One clear view.

The redesign restructured the dashboard around three core user modes: Monitor, Analyse, and Act. A customisable widget system with smart defaults let users pin the metrics that mattered to them — while a new contextual layer added brief explanations and trend arrows to every key figure.

The dark-mode-first visual direction reduced eye strain during extended use sessions and gave the product a strong, distinctive identity in a crowded SaaS market.

Outcomes

Results that
moved the needle.

Three months post-launch, Makins saw meaningful improvement across every key metric — validating the decision to prioritise clarity over feature density.

38% Increase in daily active users within 90 days of launch
2 min Average time-to-insight, down from 4.2 minutes
67 NPS score post-launch, up from 31
