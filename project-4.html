Case Study — 04

Bingo

Leading the end-to-end design of a bold fintech product — from brand identity and information architecture through to high-fidelity UI and a successful App Store launch.

Role Lead Product Designer
Timeline 16 Weeks
Platform iOS · Android
Year 2023
A new fintech product doesn't just need to work — it needs a reason to exist. Bingo was designed with a distinct point of view: fast, bold, and built for people who expect more from their money.
The Challenge

Standing out in
a crowded space.

The fintech market is saturated. Dozens of apps offer the same core features — send, receive, track, save. For Bingo to carve out a meaningful position, the product needed to feel distinctly different from day one: not just visually, but in the way it moved, responded, and talked to its users.

The Problem

The founding team had a clear market thesis but no design language, no validated user flows, and no component foundation. Everything needed to be built — and it needed to be built fast, without sacrificing quality or coherence.

Design Goals

  • Define a distinct visual identity from scratch
  • Design core flows completable in under 3 taps
  • Build a component system alongside the product
  • Ship to App Store within 4 months
Strategy & Research

Defining the space
before filling it.

I began with a competitive teardown of 12 fintech apps — mapping their onboarding flows, interaction patterns, and visual language. The goal wasn't to copy the best, but to identify where the entire category was making the same wrong assumptions.

A brand workshop with the founding team established the product's personality: bold, trustworthy, and fast. Purple emerged from multiple iterations as the anchor colour — energetic without being reckless, modern without feeling cold.

Research Methods

  • Competitive teardown of 12 fintech apps
  • Brand positioning workshop with founders
  • 3 rounds of usability testing on prototypes
  • 20 concept interviews with target users

Strategic Decisions

  • Card-first UI to mirror how users think about money
  • Colour as the primary brand differentiator
  • Speed as a core UX principle, not a nice-to-have
  • Transparency in every transaction display
The Solution

Bold by design.
Fast by principle.

Bingo launched with a purple identity that felt premium and distinctive. The card-first transaction layout mapped directly to how users mentally organise their finances — each transaction a clear, readable row with no ambiguity about direction or amount.

Every primary action was designed to require no more than three taps. Motion design reinforced a sense of speed and responsiveness throughout — the app didn't just look fast, it felt fast. Components were built in parallel with screens, giving engineering a system they could confidently build from day one.

Outcomes

Shipped, loved,
featured.

Bingo launched on schedule and surpassed early download targets within the first week — a result of a design process that balanced speed with rigour.
